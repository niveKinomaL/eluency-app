App design:

Modernize Visual Design
•	Embrace Liquid Glass on iOS. Use translucent cards with rounded corners and subtle depth to highlight content, aligning with Apple’s Liquid Glass aesthetic. Add gentle motion effects when switching between tabs or opening modals.
•	Material You for Android. Allow color theming to adapt to the user’s wallpaper; provide dark mode and ensure high contrast for text. Use Material 3 components (cards, buttons, modal sheets) and micro animations to guide users.
•	Consistent icons & typography. Use SF Pro for iOS and Roboto or Google’s new system font for Android, maintaining minimum 17 pt body text. Follow iconography guidelines with simple, meaningful icons.
•	Improve imagery. Ensure each vocabulary card includes a high quality image that clearly illustrates the concept. Avoid overly abstract pictures; research shows that pictorial representations paired with words enhance memory
•	Offline access: Allow lessons and practice sets to be downloaded for offline use, important for students without reliable internet.

•	Localization: Translate UI text and microcopy into the learners’ first language. Clear microcopy reduces cognitive load and fosters trust

Student/Teacher/Admin Login:
•	Persistent Login: Once users log in, they remain logged in unless they choose to log out.
•	Admin Notifications: Admin accounts can send push notifications directly to users’ phones.
•	Teacher Lesson & Test Distribution: Teachers can create and push lessons or tests and add students from the app. Students receive a notification on their app when new lessons or tests are added to their account.
•	Completion Notifications: Teachers receive a notification when a student completes a lesson or test, when clicked takes them to that lesson/test the student completed.
•	Teacher Account Management: Teachers can sign up, manage their platform, and create lessons or tests directly from their phones. They can also subscribe through Apple Pay or Google Pay.
